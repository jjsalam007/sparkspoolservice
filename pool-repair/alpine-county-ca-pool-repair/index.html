Regular Cleaning

On a daily basis, remember to skim the water surface to remove leaves, debris, and bugs. Employ a vacuum cleaner designed for pools to regularly clean the pool's bottom, as well as scrub the walls and tiles to prevent the formation of algae.

Maintain Water Chemistry

Don't forget to perform regular water testing and adjust the necessary chemical levels to sustain proper pH, alkalinity, as well as chlorine/bromine levels. This practice is essential for preventing the development of algae as well as bacteria.

Shock Treatment

Consistently, apply a treatment to shock the pool using either chlorine or a non-chlorine option. This practice is crucial for eliminating any resistant bacteria and algae to the typical chlorination.
