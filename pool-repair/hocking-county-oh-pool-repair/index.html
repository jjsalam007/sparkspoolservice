Regular Cleaning

On a daily basis, remember to skim the water surface to remove leaves, debris, and bugs. Employ a pool vacuum for consistent cleaning the bottom of the pool, as well as scrub the walls and tiles to thwart the formation of algae.

Regularly, follow the manufacturer's recommendations to maintain or replace your filter cartridge, or perform backwashing for sand or DE filters. This maintenance guarantees optimal water circulation by ensuring the filter is free of debris.

Maintain Water Chemistry

Always keep in mind to perform regular water testing and make necessary adjustments to maintain proper pH, alkalinity, as well as chlorine/bromine levels. This practice plays a crucial role in staving off algae as well as bacteria.

Shock Treatment

On a regular basis, apply a treatment to shock the pool with either chlorine or a non-chlorine option. This practice is crucial to eradicate any algae and bacteria that may be resistant to regular chlorination methods.
