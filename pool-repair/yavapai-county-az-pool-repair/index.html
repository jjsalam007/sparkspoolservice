Pool Maintenance Tips

Regular Cleaning

Each day, make it a habit to skim the pool's surface to remove leaves, debris, and insects. Use a vacuum cleaner designed for pools to regularly clean the bottom of the pool, and scrub the walls and tiles to thwart the formation of algae.

Check Pump and Motor

Regularly, adhere to the manufacturer's guidelines to clean or replace the filter cartridge, or backwash the sand or DE filter. These actions guarantees optimal water circulation by keeping the filter clean.

Maintain Water Chemistry

Don't forget to perform regular water testing and adjust the necessary chemical levels to maintain the correct pH, alkalinity, and chlorine/bromine levels. Doing so is essential for staving off algae and bacteria.

Shock Treatment

On a regular basis, administer a treatment to shock the pool using either chlorine or a non-chlorine option. This practice is crucial to eradicate any algae and bacteria that may be resistant to regular chlorination methods.
