Pool Maintenance Tips

Regular Cleaning

Each day, make it a habit to skim the water surface to eliminate leaves, particles, and insects. Use a vacuum cleaner designed for pools to regularly clean the bottom of the pool, and scrub the walls and tiles to thwart the growth of algae.

Check Pump and Motor

Periodically, follow the manufacturer's recommendations to maintain or replace your filter cartridge, or backwash the sand or DE filter. This maintenance ensures effective water circulation by ensuring the filter is free of debris.

Maintain Water Chemistry

Always keep in mind to test the water consistently and make necessary adjustments to sustain proper pH, alkalinity, and chlorine/bromine levels. This practice plays a crucial role in staving off algae and bacteria.

Shock Treatment

Consistently, administer a treatment to shock the pool using either chlorine or a non-chlorine option. This step is essential for eliminating any algae and bacteria that may be resistant to regular chlorination methods.
